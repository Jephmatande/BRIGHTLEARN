## BRIGHTLEARN COFFEE SHOP ANALYSIS

## THE DATA ANALYSIS PROJECT
The data analysis project originated from the instructions from the new CEO of Bright Coffee Shop. The instruction sought to gain insight into sales and revenue across three business locations: Hell's Kitchen, Astoria, and Lower Manhattan. These findings would ensure better decision-making. The data analysis was intended to solicit information on the best-selling locations among the three places, customer purchasing behaviours, best performing products, and the peak selling periods. The data analysis process followed a logical outline from understanding the problem, extracting the dataset, data uploading and cleaning, exploratory data analysis, data analysis, presentation, and reporting. Various tools were used in the process, including Miro for midmap and planning the project, Databricks for cleaning, exploring, and enhancing the data, MS Excel for analysing and visualising the data, as well as Canva for presenting the findings.

## Project objectives
The project aimed to:
* perform an analysis of trends of products in shops in different locations, across product types, and product categories.
* Identify top-performing products the shop is offering.
* Identify revenue trends in relation to the period under study.
* Make recommendations to the CEO based on the data analysis results.

## Exploratory data analysis
The dataset has 149117 entries and covers a period of 6 months from January to June. There are no missing values. The total revenue is slightly above US$688 000. The data relates to sales from three locations, namely,
Astoria
Hell`s Kitchen and
Lower Manhattan.

The row dataset has 8 columns and includes key details such as the locations, the trading times, product ID, the unit price, and the quantity purchased. The quality of the dataset was also assessed to check for any errors as well as accuracy. The data was found to be of good quality and was ready for analysis.

## The data analysis process
The data analysis process included
a. Understanding the research purpose and project from the CEO, as well as the reason and expected outcomes of the analysis.
b. Data cleaning -Checking for missing values, checking data types and format, standadising the date and time formats
c. Data extraction, loading, and transformation. This includes data enhancement by adding more columns to the dataset. These include the time buckets, spending buckets, the time of data, and the revenue. Databricks was useful for this. The process involved the importation of the CSV dataset into Databricks and data enhancement and transformation.
d. Exploratory data analysis. This included understanding the data structure and composition.
e. Data analysis process included downloading the enhanced dataset in CSV format to MS Excel for analysis and visualisation. Pivot table played a significant role in using the different data visualisation tools. These include pie charts, bar graphs, line graphs, etc. The analysis included the following;
* Transactions by store location
Revenue by store location
* Revenue by product category and product type to identify the best and poor performing products
* Revenue by time trends (day, week, and month) and
* Peak and trough sales periods

## Key findings from the data analysis

The analysis revealed some key business insights to promote strategic decision-making. These include;

* A total of about 149 100 transaction in the three locations generated  US$698,812 in total revenue across all three store locations.
* The best-selling location in Hell`s Kitchen is at 34%, while the other two trail closely behind at about 33% each.
* Mornings registered the busiest sales with about 64%, while evenings are the least busy. 
* The data shows a steady increase in revenue from January to June, with the lowest sales in February and the highest in June.
* Sales remained relatively consistent throughout the month, with mid-month contributing the highest revenue (34.8%) and early month contributing the lowest (31.4%).
* The majority (about 99%) of customers are low spenders.

  ## Key recommendations

The following recommendations emerge from the data:

* Increase the number of outlets in other locations to encourage more sales.
* Staff training for peak sales hours.
* Improve social media visibility and digital marketing of products.
* Introduce online shops for the products.
* Maintain adequate inventory for high-demand products.
* Conduct promotions, combos, and freebies to push the marketing of low-performing products.
* Introduce targeted marketing campaigns during off-peak times, e.g., evenings and month-end and winter times.
* Review pricing or product offerings for consistently low-performing items.
* Frequent data analysis spread over every three months to keep tracking the changes for quick decision making.

  ## Conclusion
 The analysis conducted on the Bright Coffee dataset has revealed that the three locations contribute equally to the revenue. There are three high-performing
products, while others are low-performing. Revenue differs with the time of the day, the time of the month, and the season. Different recommendations, including marketing, automation, staff training, and product promotion have been provided.
* Best sellers are coffee, tea and bakery products. Worst performers are grean tea, grean beans and brewed coffee with a total contribution of about 1% of the revenue.
