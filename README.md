# BRIGHTLEARN COFFEE SHOP ANALYSIS

##THE DATA ANALYSIS PROJECT
The data analysis project originated from the instruction from the new CEO of Bright Coffee Shop. The instruction sought to gain insight on sales and revenue from the business`s three locations namely, Hell`s Kitchen, Astoria and Lower Manhattan. These findings would ensure better decision making. The data analysis was intended at soliciting information on the best selling locations among the three places, customer purchasing behaviours, best performing products and the peak selling periods. The data analysis process followed a logical outline from understanding the problem, extracting the dataset, data uploading and cleaning, exploratory data analysis, data analysis, preesentation and reporting. Various tools were used in the process including Miro for workbreakdown and planning the project, Databricks for cleaning, exploring and enhancing the data, MS Excel for analysing and visualising the data as well as Canva for presenting the findings.

## project objectives
The project aimed to:
* perform ana analysis of trends of products in shops in different locations, accross product types, and product categories.
* Identify top-performing products the shop is offering.
* Identify revenue trendes in relation to the period under study.
* Make recommendations to the CEO based on the data analysis results.

## Exploratory data analaysis
The dataset has 149117 entries and covers a period of 6 months from January to June. There are no missing values. The total revenue is slightly above US$688 000. The data relates to sales from three locations namely,
Astoria
Hell`s Kitchen and
Lower Manhattan.

The row dataset has 8 columns and includes key details such as the locations, the trading times, product ID, the unit price and the quantity purchased. The quality of the dataset was also assessed to chack for any errors as well as accuracy. The data was found to be of good quality and was ready for analysis.

## Key findings from the data analysis

The analysis revealed some key business insights to promote strategic decision making. These include;

* A total of about 149 100 transaction in the three locations generated  US$698,812 in total revenue across all three store locations.
* The best selling location in Hell`s Kitchen at 34% while the other two trail closely behind at about 33% each.
* The data shows a stead increase in revenue from January to June with the lowets sales in February and the highest in June.
Sales remained relatively consistent throughout the month, with mid-month contributing the highest revenue (34.8%) and early month contributing the lowest (31.4%).
* Morning and mid-morning recorded the highest customer activity and sales volume.
* A small number of products (Coffee and Tea) generated a significant proportion of overall revenue.
* Several products consistently underperformed, presenting opportunities for sales improvement.
* Revenue patterns were consistent across multiple months, indicating stable customer demand.
* Store performance varied across product categories, suggesting location-specific customer preferences.
